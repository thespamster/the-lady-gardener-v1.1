Hi, I'm Tracey the Gardengal and welcome to my website. I have lived in and around Buckfastleigh in the beautiful county of Devon for quite a few years now and inspired by the countryside I decided to change my life and become a gardener.

Gardening is such a wonderful way to spend my days and I love helping people achieve their garden dreams. Plus there's always time for a nice cup of tea and maybe a suggestion of cake.

Feel free to check out the galleries below where there's pictures of some of my favourite flowers and plants plus a few photos of my garden build. Reach out to me via Facebook, Email or Instagram using the icons below if you'd like to chat about your garden.

Dianthus - Dianthus

Known as garden pinks because their leaves look like they've been trimmed with pinking shears.

Acer

Also known as the Japanese maple they are actually trees and are ideal to grow in containers.

Azalea - Rhododendron

Once thought to be entirely separate to rhododendrons azaleas are now considered to be part of the rhododendron family.

Sunflower - Helianthus

Sunflowers are iconic and as one of the easiest flowers to grow they thrive in full sun and aren’t picky about soil.

Pyracantha - Pyracantha

A very pretty evergreen shrub also known as Firethorn. Grows flowers and berries which can be red, yellow or orange.

Cosmos - Cosmos

Cosmos are sun-loving plants with a long flowering season that runs well into autumn.

Rose - Rosa

Roses are a classic and instantly recognisable plant, many are fragrant, ideal for almost every style of garden.

Hellebore - Helleborus

A classic winter flowering plant but longlasting so you'll still see them in spring.

Echinacea - Echinacea

Also known as coneflower these plants are again very popular with pollinators and have very distinctive flowers that bring a lovely splash of colour to any garden.
